The past tense Passé Composé

When Do You Use Passé Composé in French?

In French, Passé composé is used to talk about the past, especially to describe actions that took place and were completed in the past.

It often corresponds to two different tenses in English: the past simple (I worked, you met, he saw) and the present perfect ( have worked, you've met, he has seen).

Take a look at these examples:

Il a acheté une maison. (He bought a house. / He has bought a house.)

Je suis allé au cinéma. (I went to the cinema.)

Elle a perdu ses clés. (She lost her keys. / She has lost her keys.)

To form Passé Composé you'll need 2 parts:

  • the present tense of the verb avoir (meaning to have) or être (meaning to be),
  • a part of the main verb called the past participle (acheté, allé, vu, etc.).
