Verb conjugation The present tense Group 1

Examples of Verbs Conjugated in the Present Tense (group 1)

Let's look at a few examples of verbs conjugated in the present tense in French:

  • J'aime la musique. (I like music.)
  • Je cherche du travail. (I'm looking for a job.)
  • Je parle anglais. (I speak English.)
  • Tu aimes la musique. (You like music.)
  • Tu cherches du travail. (You're looking for a job.)
  • Tu parles anglais. (You speak English.)
  • Il / elle / on aime la musique. (He / she / one likes music.)
  • Il / elle / on cherche du travail. (He / she / one is looking for a job.)
  • Il / elle / on parle anglais. (He / she / one speaks English.)
  • Nous aimons la musique. (We like music.)
  • Nous cherchons du travail. (We're looking for a job.)
  • Nous parlons anglais. (We speak English.)
  • Vous aimez la musique. (You like music.)
  • Vous cherchez du travail. (You're looking for a job.)
  • Vous parlez anglais. (You speak English.)
  • Ils/elles aiment la musique. (They like music.)
  • Ils/elles cherchent du travail. (They're looking for a job.)
  • Ils/elles parlent anglais. (They speak English.)

More verbs that follow the same pattern:

chanter (to sing), marcher (to walk), regarder (to watch), demander (to ask), commencer (to start), manger (to eat), travailler (to work), habiter (to live), écouter (to listen).

Most verbs with an infinitive ending in "-er" are commonly known as first-group verbs.

To conjugate a first-group verb in the present tense, follow these steps:

Remove the infinitive ending (-er) to get the stem. Example: "parler" becomes "parl-". Add the appropriate ending to the stem. You'll find the endings in the table below.

SUBJECT ENDING EXAMPLE
je -e parle
tu -es parles
il/elle/on -e parle
nous -ons parlons
vous -ez parlez
ils/elles -ent parlent

Pronunciation tip

For verbs that follow the pattern above, the present tense endings '-e', '-es' and '-ent' are not pronounced, so 'parle', 'parles', and 'parlent' have the same pronunciation.

Note that when a verb starts with a vowel and is conjugated with "je" in the present tense, the "e" from "je" is removed:

  • - instead of "je aime", you say "j'aime",
  • - instead of "je écoute", you say "j'écoute".

Verbs with Spelling Changes

Let's look at some -er verbs that follow a similar pattern as the verbs above, but undergo some spelling changes often related to pronunciation and spelling rules.

Common patterns and examples of verbs that follow these patterns

Pattern 1: acheter, (se) lever, amener, emmener...

  • J'achète des vêtements. (I buy clothes.)
  • Je me lève à 8 heures. (I wake up at 8 o'clock.)
  • Tu achètes des vêtements. (You buy clothes.)
  • Tu te lèves à 8 heures. (You wake up at 8 o'clock.)
  • Il / elle / on achète des vêtements. (He / she / one buys clothes.)
  • Il / elle / on se lève à 8 heures. (He / she / one wakes up at 8 o'clock.)
  • Nous achetons des vêtements. (We buy clothes.)
  • Nous nous levons à 8 heures. (We wake up at 8 o'clock.)
  • Vous achetez des vêtements. (You buy clothes.)
  • Vous vous levez à 8 heures. (You wake up at 8 o'clock.)
  • Ils/elles achètent des vêtements. (They buy clothes.)
  • Ils/elles se lèvent à 8 heures. (They wake up at 8 o'clock.)

Pattern 2: espérer, répéter, accéder, compléter...

  • J'espère arriver à l'heure. (I hope to arrive on time.)
  • Je préfère la pizza. (I prefer pizza.)
  • Tu espères arriver à l'heure. (You hope to arrive on time.)
  • Tu préfères la pizza. (You prefer pizza.)
  • Il / elle / on espère arriver à l'heure. (He / She / One hopes to arrive on time.)
  • Il / elle / on préfère la pizza. (He / She / One prefers pizza.)
  • Nous espérons arriver à l'heure. (We hope to arrive on time.)
  • Nous préférons la pizza. (We prefer pizza.)
  • Vous espérez arriver à l'heure. (You hope to arrive on time.)
  • Vous préférez la pizza. (You prefer pizza.)
  • Ils/elles espèrent arriver à l'heure. (They hope to arrive on time.)
  • Ils/elles préfèrent la pizza. (They prefer pizza.)

Pattern 3: appeler, s'appeler, jeter...

  • J'appelle son frère. (I call his brother.)
  • Tu appelles son frère. (You call his brother.)
  • Il/ elle/ on appelle son frère. (He / She / One calls his brother.)
  • Nous appelons son frère. (We call his brother.)
  • Vous appelez son frère. (You call his brother.)
  • Ils/elles appellent son frère. (They call his brother.)
