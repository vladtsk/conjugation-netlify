Verb conjugation Passé Composé 'Avoir' VS 'être'

The past tense Passé Composé

Verbs conjugated with 'avoir' and 'être'

In French, most verbs form their Passé Composé with avoir: "j'ai regardé", "elle a donné", "il a acheté", etc.

There are two main groups of verbs that form their perfect tense with 'être' instead of avoir:

  • all reflexive verbs (verbs with 'se' in the infinitive form: se lever, se coucher, se raser…)
  • a small group of French verbs that are mainly used to talk about movement or a change of some kind (see the table below).
Common French verbs conjugated with 'être'
Verb Translation Conjugation example
aller to go Je suis allé.
venir to come Elle est venue.
revenir to come back Nous sommes revenus.
arriver to arrive Ils sont arrivés.
entrer to enter Il est entré.
rentrer to come (back) home Tu es rentré.
partir to leave Elle est partie.
rester to stay Je suis resté.
sortir to go out Ils sont sortis.
retourner to go back Nous sommes retournés.
tomber to fall Vous êtes tombé.

A few more verbs conjugated with 'être': devenir (to become), monter (to go up), descendre (to go down), passer (to go past, to pass (by)), naître (to be born), mourir (to die).

Note that the verbs passer, entrer, sortir, monter, retourner can also be conjugated with 'avoir' in some cases.
