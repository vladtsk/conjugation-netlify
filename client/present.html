The present tense (le présent de l'indicatif)

When Do You Use the Present Tense in French?

In French, the simple present tense (le présent de l'indicatif) is often used to describe an action that is happening now, a habit, a general fact/truth or a future plan.

Here are a few examples:

Je regarde un film. (I'm watching a film.)

Il travaille tous les jours. (He works every day.)

Elle parle trois langues. (She speaks three languages.)

Vous arrivez à 16h. (You arrive at 4pm.)
