Verb conjugation Intro

Introduction to French conjugation and tenses

What is verb conjugation?

Verb conjugation refers to changing a verb to express different aspects, such as when the action happens, who is performing the action, whether the action is completed or ongoing.

For example, if you want to express "I work" or "I'm working", you'll use: je travaille. For "you work" / "you're working" (singular, informal), you'd use: tu travailles. And for "he works" / "he's working", the correct phrase is: il travaille.

So you can't just say "je travailler", "tu travailler", etc. You have to conjugate the verb, so in this case you have to change the ending according to the subject (the person).

What is an infinitive?

Each verb has an infinitive form, which typically have one of these endings: "-er", "-ir", or "-re". It's the form of a verb that has not been conjugated and that you can find in a dictionary.

For example, parler (to speak), finir (to finish), attendre (to wait).
