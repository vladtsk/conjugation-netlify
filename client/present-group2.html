Examples of Verbs Conjugated in the Present Tense (group 2)

Let's look at another pattern commonly referred to as 2nd group verbs.

  • Je choisis un film. (I choose a movie.)
  • Je finis mes études. (I finish my studies.)
  • Tu choisis un film. (You choose a movie.)
  • Tu finis tes études. (You finish your studies.)
  • Il / elle choisit un film. (He / She chooses a movie.)
  • Il / elle finit ses études. (He / She finishes his / her studies.)
  • Nous choisissons un film. (We choose a movie.)
  • Nous finissons nos études. (We finish our studies.)
  • Vous choisissez un film. (You choose a movie.)
  • Vous finissez vos études. (You finish your studies.)
  • Ils/elles choisissent un film. (They choose a movie.)
  • Ils/elles finissent leurs études. (They finish their studies.)

Some common verbs that follow this pattern: finir (to finish), choisir (to choose), réfléchir (to think), grandir (to grow), réussir (to succeed), grossir (to gain weight), investir (to invest), remplir (to fill (in)), atterrir (to land).

More examples

  • Tu finis à quelle heure ? (What time do you finish?)
  • Je finis tard. (I finish late. / I'm finishing late.)
  • Il réussit toujours. (He always succeeds.)

Here is a table explaining how to conjugate a 2nd group verb

SUBJECT ENDING EXAMPLE
je -is choisis
tu -is choisis
il/elle/on -it choisit
nous -issons choisissons
vous -issez choisissez
ils/elles -issent choisissent

Note that not all verbs that end in "-ir" are conjugated like this. You'll see examples in the next chapter.
